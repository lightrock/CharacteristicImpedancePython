## The experimental pressure

Before Einstein's explanation, the photoelectric effect had already been observed
and studied experimentally, including by Hertz and Lenard.

The strange pressure was:

```text
below threshold frequency: no electron emission, even if intensity is increased
above threshold frequency: emission occurs
higher frequency: higher maximum electron kinetic energy
higher intensity: more emitted electrons, not higher maximum electron energy
```

The classical wave-only expectation did not fit that pattern cleanly.

## Einstein's move

Einstein's useful move was to treat light energy exchange as quantized:

```text
E = h nu
```

At a material boundary:

```text
incoming light quantum energy
= material work function
+ maximum photoelectron kinetic energy
```

Or:

```text
K_max = h nu - phi
```

where `phi` is the material work function.

This makes the threshold behavior natural:

```text
if h nu < phi:
    no emission
else:
    electron can leave, with leftover energy as kinetic energy
```
